# The Trades Lab — Brand Guidelines (v1.0)

> Cinematic websites, engineered for trades.

---

## The mark

A **plumb-bob** — the oldest tool for getting something exactly right, and a quiet nod to water (for the plumbing niche). It signals **true, level, precise**: websites that are exact, dependable and beautifully made. The hanging beam doubles as a subtle "T".

Don't redraw it. Use the supplied vector files.

## Clear space & minimum size

- **Clear space:** keep padding around the logo equal to the height of the plumb-bob's beam. Nothing intrudes.
- **Minimum size:** horizontal lockup no smaller than **140px** wide; symbol no smaller than **24px**; favicon handles 16px.

## Colour

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Trade Navy** | `#0E1B2A` | Primary — backgrounds, body text |
| **Brass** | `#C9A227` | Accent — highlights, "LAB", CTAs |
| **Spirit Teal** | `#0F766E` | Secondary — links, small UI detail |
| **Chalk** | `#F7F4EF` | Light surfaces, reversed text |

Supporting ink for long copy on light: `#3A434E`. Hairlines/dividers: `#E3DDD1`.

Rule of thumb: navy + chalk do 90% of the work; brass is the *one* thing you want the eye to land on; teal is a quiet accent — never compete with brass.

---

## Typography

- **Space Grotesk** (500 Medium, 700 Bold) — display, headlines, and the logotype.
- **Inter** (400 / 600 / 700) — body, UI, long-form copy.

Headlines: Space Grotesk Bold, tight tracking (≈ -1 to -2). Body: Inter Regular, comfortable line-height (1.5–1.6). Small labels/eyebrows: Inter SemiBold, uppercase, +2–3 letter-spacing.

Web fonts: both are free on Google Fonts / Fontsource.

---

## Do / Don't

**Do** — use the master SVGs · keep navy/brass/teal/chalk · give it room · use the reversed version on dark.

**Don't** — recolour the mark outside the palette · stretch or rotate it · add a drop shadow or gradient · place the light lockup on a busy photo (use the reversed version on a navy scrim) · recreate the wordmark in a different font.
